Pasta carbonara

A flavored and delicious dish of the italian Pasta Carbonara

Pasta carbonara is a classic Italian dish known for its simplicity and rich flavors. It consists of spaghetti or other pasta varieties, typically coated in a velvety sauce made with eggs, grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese, crispy pancetta or guanciale (Italian cured pork jowl), and black pepper. The heat from the freshly cooked pasta gently cooks the sauce, resulting in a creamy and savory combination that is both comforting and delicious.

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti or the pasta of your choice
  • Eggs
  • Grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese (or a combination of both)
  • Pancetta or guanciale (Italian cured pork jowl), diced or sliced
  • Freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Cook the Pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook the pasta according to the package instructions until it is al dente. Drain the pasta, reserving a small amount of the pasta water.
  2. Prepare the Sauce: While the pasta is cooking, whisk the eggs in a bowl and mix in the grated cheese (Pecorino Romano or Parmesan) and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. The amount of cheese and pepper can be adjusted to your taste.
  3. Cook the Pancetta/Guanciale: In a separate skillet or pan, cook the diced or sliced pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until it becomes crispy and renders its fat. If desired, you can add a little olive oil to the pan.
  4. Combine the Pasta and Pancetta/Guanciale: Once the pasta is cooked, return it to the pot (off the heat). Add the cooked pancetta/guanciale along with its rendered fat to the pasta and toss well to combine.
  5. Add the Sauce: Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta. The residual heat from the pasta will gently cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce. Toss the pasta vigorously to coat it evenly with the sauce. If the sauce appears too thick, add a small amount of the reserved pasta water and continue tossing until the desired creamy consistency is achieved.
  6. Serve: Divide the pasta carbonara among plates or bowls. Garnish with additional cheese and black pepper if desired, and optionally sprinkle with chopped parsley for added freshness.
  7. Enjoy: Serve immediately while the pasta carbonara is still warm and creamy. Savor the delicious blend of flavors and textures!
